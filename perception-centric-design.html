Perception-centric design considerations for low-cost haptic emulation in prototypes

Working with the University of Bristol Design and Manufacturing Futures Laboratory.

I carried out a research internship during the summer of 2023 working alongside PhD students in the laboratory, this started with the aim of producing a solution for integrating haptics within prototypes, the reasoning being that companies spend vast amounts producing mockups, particularly in the latter stages of the design process. Any improvements in fidelity (i.e. how believable a model is) or modularity (resulting in saving wasted material on producing new copies) are therefore highly sought after. As well as learning lots about electronics, prototyping, and haptic motors; I also explored new ways of performing dynamic analysis such as using acoustic emission. Repositories containing my code for the Arduino touch-sensor and haptics-demo (which involved a model in the Unity game engine rendered in VR, linking to the actuator in the prototype) can be found on the DMFDML Github page.

During the project I developed an interest in Human Computer Interaction (HCI) and especially the psychology and physiology behind these interactions; and carried out a thorough search through existing work new and old. I particularly enjoyed reading into human sensory systems, which I think are important to understand as they affect everything in our lives, and could be used for bio-inspired designs of the future.

I was the lead author in a conference paper resulting from the project which was presented at DESIGN 2024 in Croatia.

An early blog post from the project can be found on the laboratory website here: What are haptics? How do they work?
