Robotic arm for drawing shapes

December 2023

Given cheap DC motors instead of conventional stepper motors, the challenge in this project was designing an Arduino control system capable of extracting some performance from less than ideal hardware. The abstract was as follows:

Physical and controller optimisations were made to an existing example model of an Arduino controlled bi-articulated robot arm over approximately two months. The main design improvements were reducing arm-tip to writing-contact distance, using more rigid parts, and making them adjustable. MATLAB sensitivity analysis was carried out, finding that the best sensitivity occurred with the shortest equal length arms possible. Physical sensitivity testing was also carried out by drawing a large grid and assessing accuracy. Controller design was derived using classical mechanics and Simulink. An Output Feedback Controller was created however abandoned due to time constraints. The final controller used derived PID gains with controller deadzone, filtering, and anti-windup; as well as some reference signal adaptations. This was capable of producing the required shapes in 10-20 seconds with a maximum deviation of roughly 2%.

Arguments for adoption of the Molten Salt Fast Reactor (MSFR)

December 2023

This project involved a detailed analysis of geopolitical considerations for making a long term commitment to a nuclear power. Molten Salt Fast Reactors (MSFR) were deemed to be the best option of the Generation IV reactors:

  • They operate at higher temperatures (meaning they have a higher Carnot efficiency compared to light water reactors)
  • They operate at near atmospheric pressure (allowing thinner vessel walls)
  • No water usage (meaning no risk of steam explosions)
  • Reactor contents are kept in liquid phase (meaning no meltdowns)
There was also a detailed material selection and stress corrosion cracking failure analysis of a hexagonal tank wall weld from the proposed design.

Huygens wave modelling for non-destructive defect detection

December 2023

Defects were characterised using wave modelling in MATLAB based on Full Matrix Capture (FMC) data from an ultrasound scan of a broken part. An optimisation study was carried out to determine the optimum parameters such as centre frequency, element pitch/width/position, number of elements, and Hanning filter size. I learnt a lot about using Fast Fourier Transforms.

As a paid researcher working alongside academics in engineering and social sciences, I analysed survey responses from hundreds of students using the Braun and Clarke methodology, helped write a report for the head of department, produced a poster, and delivered a conference talk.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of cantilever beam and lifting lugs

November 2022

I carried out an analysis of a proposed design for structural marine applications involving six welded S355JR steel lugs. A sensitivity study was carried out using a variety of mesh types in order to verify that the results were converging to an accurate value. Using this a suitable Finite Element (FE) model was devised using Abaqus CAE.
