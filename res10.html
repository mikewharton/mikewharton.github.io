Perception-centric design considerations for low-cost haptic emulation in prototypes

Working with the University of Bristol Design and Manufacturing Futures Lab.

Following on from, and working alongside PhD students in the research group, I carried out a research internship during the summer of 2023. This started with the aim of producing a solution for integrating haptics within prototypes, the reasoning being that companies spend vast amounts producing mockups, particularly in the latter stages of the design process, so any improvements in fidelity (i.e. how believable a model is) or modularity (resulting in saving wasted material on producing new copies) are greatly appreciated. As well as learning lots about electronics, prototyping, and haptic motors; I explored new ways of performing dynamic analysis such as using acoustic emission.

During the project I developed an interest in Human Computer Interaction (HCI) and especially the psychology and physiology behind these interactions; and carried out a thorough search through existing work new and old. I particularly enjoyed taking out inter-library loans for old books on human sensory systems. It is important to understand these as they affect everything in our lives, and could be used for bio-inspired designs of the future.

I was the lead author in a conference paper resulting from the project which was presented at DESIGN 2024 in Croatia.
