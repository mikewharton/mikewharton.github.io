Research Experience

■ 2024 (January-April), University of Bristol

Achieved 83% in my final year group project titled “Exploring and modelling the causes and effects of dynamic chain drive vibrations” earning the Bechtel Industrial Group Project Prize for the highest average group mark in the cohort. Worked alongside RAs in the Electrical Energy Management and Composites groups combining materials, software, and experimental analysis of resonant bike chains using slow motion videogrammetry.

■ 2023 (June-September), Design and Manufacturing Futures Research Group

Alongside postgraduate students and academics in the Design and Manufacturing Futures Research Group, I carried out a paid standalone research project over four months exploring how the use of haptics could impact prototyping of the future. Work involved academic writing, coding in Python for Unity, as well as hands on control work with Arduino, ESP32, haptic motors, a laser vibrometer and 3D printing. I produced a conference paper for the 2024 International DESIGN conference in Croatia.

■ 2023 (January-April), University of Bristol

Achieved 83% in my individual research project “On the Use of Photogrammetry for Low Cost Structural Health Monitoring”. This has since been published in the Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing journal. For the project I produced a proof-of-concept software package in MATLAB capable of rudimentary dynamic image analysis, and using a video camera with a high optical zoom I demonstrated its capability for long range non-contact dynamic analysis on the Clifton suspension bridge.

■ 2022-2024 Bristol Institute for Learning and Teaching (BILT) Conference

Worked as a paid research assistant and student ambassador alongside three senior academics in engineering and social sciences. I carried out qualitative analysis of hundreds of student responses, produced an infographic poster, and delivered a solo conference presentation to ~50 attendees.
